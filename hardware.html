Hardware Experience:

My experience with hardware started since I was a little kid. I was always amazed at technology and what humans have made. I started working and looking at the insides of computers when I was around 10 years old and this is mainly due to my dads love for computers as well. He built his own computer well before I was born and I used to love learning about all these old parts that no one used anymore. I used all my knowledge to build my own computers when I was older and had my own job.

Projects:

The first computer I ever took apart and put back together was a $300 HP pre-built. I took it apart because I wanted to learn how to build my own computer in the future when I got a job where I could actually pay for the parts I wanted. Well, when that time eventually came I was able to use the knowledge I gained previously and put it to use. I built a computer using about $1300 worth of parts and finished it perfectly with no problems.

Skills Used:

The skills I used were problem solving skills and hard work. When I took apart my first computer I wanted to do it without looking anything up online (which wasnt very smart). I had to learn everything for myself and where everything went and I believe this helped my problem solving skills when it came to building my second computer.
